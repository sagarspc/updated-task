Privacy Policy



What is a privacy policy?

A privacy policy is a statement contained on a website that details how the operators of the site will collect, store, protect, and utilize personal data provided by its users.

The definition of personal data includes names, addresses (physical or e-mail), IP addresses, telephone numbers, date of birth, and financial information, such as debit or credit card details.

In addition to outlining how the company will use the information, it also includes how it will meet its legal obligations, and how those sharing their data can seek recourse should the company fail to meet those responsibilities.

  • Drafting your Privacy Policy:

Any site that gathers any data about its users, even if it is simply through tracking their location, is required to have a privacy policy.

Many websites require site users to indicate that they have read the policy when they first provide their information.

Why do we need a privacy policy?

Following are the reasons that we need a privacy policy:

• A carefully drafted, easy to understand, and maybe even entertaining Privacy Policy inspires trust in clients, encouraging them to sign up with you and stick around for a while. It makes users feel safe and cared for, and this inspires faith in your product.

• A Privacy Policy teaches (or reminds) people that they have rights worth protecting.

• A Privacy Policy clearly lays out where your boundaries are when it comes to the data you use and how you plan to use it.

• A good Privacy Policy will protect you from fines and other penalties.


Following elements are to be included in the privacy policy:

• The exact information that will be collected from website users, which may include names, physical or e-mail addresses, IP addresses, and telephone numbers, and location tracking.

• If cookies are being used on the site, how to opt-out of them, and what effect this might have on the user's experience.

• How the information will be collected, and by whom, for example, if it is being collected by an advertising program.

Following points to be observed while drafting Privacy Policy:

• When creating a Privacy Policy for your business, it helps to understand and consider the spirit of the law and not just the letter.

• Your Privacy Policy exists first and foremost to protect client confidentiality.

• Keep it separate from any other legal agreements you may have. Make sure it has its own section and is referenced as such.

• Be accurate (and current) regarding what information you collect, why you collect it, what you’re using it for, and with which third parties you’ll be sharing it.

• Make your Privacy Policy understandable.
