Every supplier of goods and/or services is required to obtain registration in the State/ UT from where he makes the taxable supply if his aggregate turnover exceeds the following threshold limit in the FY:

Limit State
Rs. 10 Lakhs for both goods and services • Manipur • Mizoram • Nagaland • Tripura
Rs. 20 Lakhs for both goods and services • Arunachal Pradesh • Meghalaya • Sikkim • Uttarakhand • Puducherry • Telangana
Rs. 20 Lakhs for services and Rs. 40 Lakhs for goods • Jammu and Kashmir • Assam • Himachal Pradesh • All other states

The GST late filing penalty/ interest payment has been specified as follows:

• A person fails to furnish details of outward or inward supplies, monthly return or final return by the due date – The GST penalty for late filing is INR 100 for every day during which the failure continues, subject to a maximum of INR 5,000.

• A person fails to furnish the annual return by the due date – The GST late filing penalty is INR 100 for every day during which the failure continues, subject to a maximum of quarter percent of the person’s turnover in the state where he is registered.

• Interest is 18% per annum. It has to be calculated by the taxpayer on the amount of outstanding tax to be paid. It shall be calculated on the Net tax liability identified in the ledger at the time of payment. The time period will be from the next day of filing due date till the actual date of payment.

F.A.Q

What is GST Return?

GST return is the detail of all the sales and purchases made along with tax collected or paid.

What is the late fees for non-filing of returns?

A person fails to furnish details of outward or inward supplies, monthly return or final return by the due date – The GST penalty for late filing is INR 100 for every day during which the failure continues, subject to a maximum of INR 5,000.

A person fails to furnish the annual return by the due date – The GST late filing penalty is INR 100 for every day during which the failure continues, subject to a maximum of quarter percent of the person’s turnover in the state where he is registered.

Is there any interest for non-filing of returns?

Yes, interest is payable 18% per annum.

Is there any concept of monthly and quarterly returns?

Yes, there is a concept of monthly and quarterly returns.

Who files monthly returns?

Monthly returns needs to be filed by the regular taxpayers, foreign non-residents, input service provider, tax deductors and e-commerce operators.

Who files quarterly returns?

Quarterly returns needs to be filed by the composition taxpayers.
