Msme registration



What is MSME registration:

Any company that comes under the category of MSME needs to ensure that they are registered under the MSME category. MSME registration stands for the micro small and medium enterprises registration. MSMED Act has been launched by the government of India to support the MSME through various schemes, subsidies, and incentives. With MSME Registration, banks also provide the loans at lower rate of interests, as these MSME play an important role in the country’s economic growth. With MSME registration you also get benefit for Trademark registration.
  • Procedure
  • Documents Required :
  • Eligibility for MSME Registration:

1. What is MSME?

MSME stands for Micro Small Medium Enterprise

2. What are the criteria for getting recognized as MSME?

a. When the Enterprises are engaged in manufacture or production, processing or preservation of goods:

Sr. No. Enterprise Type Criteria
1. Micro Enterprise Investment in equipment does not exceed Rs. 10 Lakhs
2. Small Enterprise Investment in equipment is more than Rs. 10 Lakhs but does not exceed Rs. 2 Crores
3. Medium Enterprise Investment in equipment is more than Rs. 2 Crores but does not exceed Rs. 5 Crores

b. When the Enterprises are engaged in providing or rendering of services:

Sr. No. Enterprise Type Criteria
1. Micro Enterprise Investment in plant and machinery does not exceed Rs. 25 Lakhs
2. Small Enterprise Investment in plant and machinery is more than Rs. 25 Lakhs but does not exceed Rs. 5 Crores
3. Medium Enterprise Investment in plant and machinery is more than Rs. 5 Crores but does not exceed Rs. 10 Crores

3. How to register?

a. It can be either online or offline.

b. Online registration can be done on http://udyogaadhaar.org.in/ through a single form.

c. Documents required are Aadhaar No, Industry Name, Address, Bank Account Details and others.

No registration fees.

e. Once uploaded you get the registration number.

4. Benefits:

a. Major Benefits:

i. 100% Collateral Free Loans from all banks

ii. Reduction in interest rate from banks

iii. Special consideration on international trade fairs

iv. Bar Code Registration subsidy

v. Concession in electricity bills.

vi. 50% subsidy for patent registration.

b. Benefits from State Governments

i. There is a sales tax exemption in most states and purchase preference on goods produced.

c. Support services by State Government:

i. Extended credit facilities

ii. Industrial extension support and services

iii. Availability of developed sites for warehouse construction

iv. Provision of training facilities

v. Hire-purchase of machineries for use in MSME

vi. Assistance in marketing, both within the country and outside (exports)

vii. Assistance for construction of industries in underdeveloped areas

viii. Reducing the one-time guarantee fee for all loans availed in North Eastern India from 1.5% to 0.75%.

d. Benefits from Central Government:

The central government, from time to time, announces schemes to benefit MSMEs, such as the credit guarantee scheme.

e. Support services by Central Government:

i. Enhancing the optimal eligibility loan from Rs. 25 lakh to Rs. 50 lakh.

ii. Reducing the one-time guarantee for loans availed of by the MSME in North-Eastern region of India from 1.5% to 0.75%.

iii. Increasing the extent of guarantee cover from 75% to 80% for:

★ Women operated Medium and small scale enterprises

★ Micro enterprises, for loans upto 5 lakhs

★Loans obtained in North Eastern region of the country

★ Reducing the one-time guarantee fee for all loans availed in North Eastern India from 1.5% to 0.75%.

5. Can new and existing business apply for MSME registration?

Yes, both new and existing business can apply for MSME registration.


The registration procedure is as follows:

1. Login to www.udyogaadhaar.gov.in

2. Enter the personal information

3. Fill the details about the enterprise/ entity

4. Fill the data as required in the form

5. Enter your bank account details i.e. IFSC Code and Bank Account No.

6. Enter the classification of your enterprise

7. Enter the no. of employees

8. Enter the total investment

9. Enter the NIC Code


Who should get MSME Registration/ Udyog Aadhaar?

Any type of business entity can obtain MSME Registration/ Udyog Aadhaar, be it Hindu Undivided Family, Proprietorship, One-Person Company, Partnership Firm, Private Limited Company, Public Limited Company, Limited Liability Partnership, Association of Persons (AOP), or any other business entity.


• Business Address Proof

• Copies of sales bill and purchase bill

• Partnership Deed/ MOA and AOA

• Copy of licenses and bills of machinery purchased.

• For firms that are listed in Schedule -III of the Industrial Licensing Exemption Notification, no Industrial license is needed. Every other enterprise must obtain it for the PRC (Provisional Registration Certificate) application.

• For the registration and document process, all statutory and administrative clearances need to be obtained such an NoC from pollution board to prove that no restrictions are being violated.


Advantages of MSME Registration:

• Excise exemption

• Exemption under direct tax laws.

• Fee reduction for filing patent and trademark.

• Credit Guarantee Scheme.

• Eligibility for availing government scheme benefits which will include loans without guarantee, low-interest rates on loan, and easy loan.

• Financial backing from the government for participating in foreign business expos.

• Enjoyment of other forms of subsidies that shall help in increasing company’s revenue.

• Concession in electricity bills.

• Exemption in government tenders.

• Refrain from asking for security deposits.

• Increased chances in winning government tenders.

• Easy and cheaper bank loans.

• Reimbursement under the bar code registration.

• Industrial promotion subsidy.


Following qualifies for known as Micro Enterprise:

Manufacturing Sector:

Investment of less than Rs. 25 lakhs in plant and machinery.

Service Sector:

Investment of less than Rs. 10 lakhs invested.


Following qualifies for known as Small Enterprise:

Manufacturing Sector:

Investment of between Rs. 25 lakhs and Rs. 5 Crores in plant and machinery.

Service Sector:

Investment of between Rs. 10 lakhs and Rs. 2 Crores in plant and machinery.


Following qualifies for known as Medium Enterprise:

Manufacturing Sector:

Investment of between Rs. 5 Crores and Rs. 10 Crores in plant and machinery.

Service Sector:

Investment of between Rs. 2 Crores and Rs. 5 Crores in plant and machinery.


F.A.Q

  • 1. How to apply for MSME Registration?

    Although, there is no compulsion to get MSME registration but is always advised to do so as it provides lots of benefits to the enterprises registered under MSME Act. It will save you from a lot of inconveniences later on.

  • 2. Is it mandatory or voluntary?

    MSME registration is totally voluntary. However, it is advisable to take registration under MSME.

  • 3. How does MSME registration help me getting a loan from bank?

    MSME’s are recognised by all the institutions and banks. They have special schemes drafted to recognise them as well and usually for providing loans banks prefer MSMEs rather than normal enterprises.

  • 4. What are the documents required for MSME registration?

    The following documents are required for MSME registration:

    Pvt. Ltd. Co./ Public Ltd. Co./OPC:

    • Company PAN Card

    • MOA, AOA & Incorporation Certificate

    • Company Address Proof


    LLP & Partnership Firm:

    • Firm PAN Card

    • LLP Agreement/ Partnership Deed

    • Firm Address Proof


    Proprietorship Firm:

    • PAN Card of proprietor

    • Shop Act

    • Address Proof of Firm


    5. What are the difference between MSME, SSI & Udyog Aadhaar?

    All the three are the same and there is no difference amongst the three.

  • 5. Do we need to file any returns?

    Yes, we need to file returns which are as follows:

    1. Every licensee shall on or before 31st May of each year, submit a return electronically or in physical form as may be prescribed by the concerned Food Safety Commissioner, in ‘Form D-1’ in respect of each class of food products handled by him during the previous financial year.

    2. Every licensee engaged in manufacturing of milk and/or milk products shall file half yearly returns for the periods 1st April to 30th September and 1st October to 31st March of every financial year in the Form D-2.

  • 6. What is the penalty for non-filing of returns?

    Any delay in filing return beyond 31st May of each year shall attract a penalty of Rs 100 per day of delay.

  • 7. Are different License required to carry out different kind of food business activities at the same premise?

    FSSAI license is based on premise. All kinds of food business activities which are being carried out at the same premise can be applied for in a single FSSAI license.

  • 8. My License/Registration is suspended or cancelled. Do I need to stop food business activities?

    On the suspension or cancellation of his/her License or registration, a food business operator needs to stop food business activities immediately. Carrying out any food business activity on suspended or cancelled License/Registration is an unlawful activity and shall attract penal actions under FSS Act, 2006.

  • 9. My License was cancelled. Can I apply for fresh License?

    After a period of 3 months from the date of cancellation, the Food Business Operator may make a fresh application for Registration or license to the concerned authority if all observations made in the improvement notice have been complied with.

  • 10. I have closed down my food business. How to surrender the FSSAI License/ Registration?

    You need to file an online application to surrender the License or Registration with reasons to be recorded. After approval of the concerned authority, it will be surrendered.

  • 11. How to transfer the License from one owner to another in case of death of owner?

    License can be transferred in case of death of the owner. Transfer application can be made online. For transferring of License, Death Certificate or Proof of Legal Heir is required.

  • 12. We manufacture only food additives. Do I need a license from FSSAI too?

    Yes, FSSAI license is required for any type of food activities including manufacturing, trading, distribution and transportation. Therefore, you need license from FSSAI.

  • 13. Is it mandatory for 100% export units to get FSSAI License?

    Yes, FSSAI license is mandatory for both export and import of food products. You will need to apply for FSSAI Central License for your export unit.

  • 14. For how many years I can renew my FSSAI license?

    FSSAI license can be renewed for 1 year up to maximum 5 year at a time.

  • 15. What is the maximum time for grant of FSSAI License after applying?

    A license has to be issued by the Licensing Authority within 60 days of making complete application by an applicant. This time includes scrutinization of application and inspection of premises by the concerned authority.

  • 16. I am holding two FSSAI License, one State License and One Central for my food business unit. Is it correct to hold two licenses at single premise?

    FSSAI License is purely premised based. No FBO is allowed to possess more than one license for single premise.

    All kinds of business shall be linked to single License obtained for single premise. If you are holding one State License and one Central License, you shall surrender the State License and apply for modification of Central License to add Kind of Business mentioned in your State License.

  • 17. My FSSAI License number has changed. Can I use the pre-printed packaging material with older FSSAI License number?

    In case of change of name, address or License number, you can apply for permission to use pre-printed packaging material. The request for permission shall be sent to FSSAI HQ with the prescribed fee.

  • 18. Who requires a Food License?

    If the business falls under the following categories, then a food license is mandatory:

    • Procurement

    • Manufacture

    • Distribution

    • Processing

    • Packaging

    • Storage

    More detailed list is as follows:

    • Hotels

    • Wholesaler

    • Restaurants

    • Food Chains

    • Food Sellers and Re-sellers

    • Dairy and dairy processing

    • Food importers and exporters

    • Processors like pickle and dry fruit maker

    • Raw material suppliers to food businesses

    • Retailers & establishments who have a retail outlet

    • Simple transporters who transfer items from one location to another

    • Canteens in corporations, schools, colleges, hospitals or government institutions

    • Packaged food manufacturers who produce items such as biscuits and ready to eat product
