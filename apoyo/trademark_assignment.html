What is a trademark



Trademark assignment:

Assignment of trade mark involves transfer of ownership of the trade mark to another person or entity with or without the goodwill of the business. A mark may be assigned or transferred to another entity in any of the following manners:

• Complete Assignment to another entity

• Assignment to another entity but with respect to only some of the goods/ services

• Assignment with goodwill

• Assignment without goodwill

  • Documents Required :

The Trademark Renewal can be done in two ways-


• You can apply renewal to change any sign or words in the already existing trademark or\


• You can apply otherwise without a change in trademark


The procedure is as follows:

Step 1: Make an application in Form TM-R

Step 2: The application shall be filed either by the registered owner or an agent authorised by him

Step 3: After filing the application, make sure to check the application status periodically unless it is processed by the Trademark registry.

F.A.Q

  • 1. What is trademark renewal?

    Trademark renewal is a process to renew your trademark again after the expiry of 10 years.

  • 2. What is the process if renewal of trademark after the expiry of the trademark registration?

    If you forgot to file your trademark renewal, then you can still do the trademark renewal anytime within the period of 6 months after of the expiry of the previous period. Trademark renewal application can be filed with form TM-R with the prescribed renewal late fees.

  • 3. When do you require to renew your trademark?

    The trademark has a validity of 10 years after which you need to renew the registration. One can register within 6 months before expiration, after six months of expiration or within one year of expiration

  • 4. Can a trademark be restored after its removal from the register?/a>

    Yes, in case the trademark is removed from the register the owner can still restore it by filing TM-10 and TM-13 forms along with the prescribed fee within one year of the trademark expiry.

  • 5. What are the consequences for non-renewal of a Trademark within the time limit?

    If the owner does not apply for renewal of the trademark within the time limit, the trademark shall be removed from the register.

  • 6. Can the trademark be removed from the registry on the basis of non-use?

    Yes, if a trademark is not in use for 5 consecutive years from the date of registration or not used for 3 months before the date of application for removal, then it can be removed.

  • 7. If trademark renewal is not done within due time and another company registers the same. Can the former owner oppose the registration?

    No, the former trademark owner does not have the right to oppose the registration since he/she does not own the particular trademark.

    Once a trademark expires, it becomes available in the public domain, and anyone can apply it for registration. So it is very essential for the trademark owner to apply for renewal

  • 8. Can the class of the trademark be changed during renewal?

    No, once a trademark has been registered in a particular class, the same cannot be changed during renewal.

  • 9. Can an objection be raised during trademark renewal?

    Yes, objection can be raised during trademark renewal.

  • 10. Is it necessary that only the owner can apply for trademark renewal?

    No, it is not necessary that only the owner can apply for trademark renewal. Trademark Renewal can also be done by authorised agent, trustee, executor or administrator.
